Autism!

Autism...

The diagnosis of autism is a bombshell—a shocking, frightening, and painful revelation to a parent. That one word from a health care professional as a formal diagnosis can be devastating for a parent about their child. Other more formal diagnosis terms are: Autism Spectrum Disorder, or Pervasive Development Disorder, or Asperger’s Syndrome. A child with the diagnosis of autism can be “on the spectrum” or just “spectrum” or even “aspie” for Asperger’s Syndrome. But the terminology is less important than what it is—autism.Please realize autism is like other things about your child—their eye color, skin tone, favorite food. Autism is something about your child. But, autism is NOT whom your child is, now or shall be in the future.

Autism is Not…

The media is full of half-truths, stereotypes, mis-information, and out and out lies about autism. The important thing for a parent is to get the factual, correct information about autism in order to help love, nurture, and develop the full potential of their child. That is the main goal and purpose here—to give you an information seeker the knowledge, and facts to help you!Some simple mis-truths about autism are easily dismissed. Autism is not a mental illness, it is not cognitive impairment or a learning disorder. A son or daughter whom is autistic (note the choice of words, is and not has) will need understanding and help for their differences from other children. Autism is a diverse difference of being, consisting of three main areas: social interaction, communication, and the senses. Autism does NOT mean your child is broken, defective, bad, or wrong. Another important fact is that autism is not a disease, so having the diagnosis of autism does not imply a child will have all the characteristics with their autism. How one autistic child behaves and responds can be vastly different from another.Autism does NOT mean that you as a parent have something wrong or defective about you in your genetics, biology, or health. Also focusing on causation does not change that your child needs you now.Autism varies to different degrees of expression. An autistic child will not have all the criteria for autism. Simply put, your child is different but also unique in their autism. This variation is true for the expression of autistic behavior in a child. Hence the various media stereotypes of autism are just that—a stereotype. Autism is not a single, definable collection of traits, but a combination of behaviors from a wide spectrum, which manifest very differently. Autism is a different way of thinking, and being for your child. A different way for your child to process the world. There are many different ways of thinking and being, and autism is one of many such ways.

Knowledge is Power

The good news is you as a parent, friend, relative, are an information seeker. Here, on this site will connect as a “nexus” of information about autism to help you. With your help, guidance, patience, love, and understanding but knowing. As a parent, you can not only help your autistic child, but also become closer with your child. The Beginning…The diagnosis that your child is autistic is not the end of the world, or a death sentence. The autism diagnosis is the start, and now seeking knowledge, you will have the information for constructively and lovingly parenting your child to achieve their full potential in life. One of the first autistic persons formally diagnosed, Donald Triplett, has had a long, happy life. Do not let that word autism be a negative. Now that you know your child is autistic, you can be a positive role in your child’s life and childhood. You have an important fact about your son or daughter as they grow and develop. Let the word autism be YOUR guide in your child’s path in life.Autism is not the end, but it is the beginning for you, and your child!
